My-co-Lab did not start as a business idea.

It started with curiosity, nature and the desire to build something meaningful.

My background combines agriculture, hands-on work and a deep interest in living systems. I studied Agricultural Engineering in Portugal, specializing in Animal Science. After finishing my degree, I spent around six months searching for opportunities without success.

At that point I made a decision that changed my path.

Because I have Swiss nationality and family connections in Switzerland, I decided to take a risk and move.

I started a new chapter and completed an apprenticeship as a Gärtner Fachrichtung Baumschule. Over the following years I worked in the field and continued building practical experience with plants and landscapes.

Today I work in Gartenbau and I want to deepen my knowledge further, especially in tree care (Baumpflege).

Working with nature has become one of the central parts of my life.

What fascinates me is not only plants themselves, but also the process behind them: how they grow, gain form and become part of something larger. I enjoy seeing spaces evolve and helping create gardens that feel alive.

At the same time, I always had another side.

Since childhood I have been fascinated by computers, problem solving and building complex things from simple parts. I grew up with a strong influence from creativity and experimentation — perhaps a little inspired by MacGyver.

The idea that you can take simple components and create something useful always fascinated me.

My interest in mushrooms began during solitary walks through the forests around Filisur.

I started noticing mushrooms and trying to understand what species I was seeing.

Simple curiosity slowly became fascination.

What fascinated me was their incredible diversity:

- shapes
- colors
- habitats
- growth patterns

But even more than that, I became fascinated by the life cycle itself.

The hidden world of mycelium.

The speed of growth.

The fact that an entire organism can exist almost invisibly and suddenly emerge.

Over time another question started growing naturally:

"How can technology help us understand and work together with nature?"

That question eventually became My-co-Lab.

My-co-Lab is an experimental laboratory where mushroom cultivation, Raspberry Pi systems, sensors, automation, programming and documentation come together.

The goal is not simply to automate mushroom cultivation.

The goal is to learn by building, document everything and share the process.

In the future I see My-co-Lab becoming a platform and company that helps people cultivate mushrooms and learn more about them.

I want it to help beginners, hobby growers and curious people who want to create, experiment and better understand the fascinating world of fungi.

I hope people who discover My-co-Lab feel curiosity.

I hope they feel inspired to build something themselves.

Because watching life grow at home is something special.
